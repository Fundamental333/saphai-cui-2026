SAPHAI : Security, Agency, Privacy of Human-AI

The rapid emergence of conversational AI and autonomous agents is reshaping how data is collected, accessed, inferred, and operationalized across human–AI ecosystems. SAPHAI positions security, agency, and privacy as mutually constitutive dimensions of human–AI interaction, not post-hoc constraints.

About

In human–AI ecosystems, security vulnerabilities can undermine autonomy, privacy failures can distort or expose identity, and constrained agency can amplify systemic risk. SAPHAI treats these as interdependent properties.

SAPHAI examines agent-mediated data exposure, identity inference, cross-modal information leakage, adversarial manipulation, secondary data use, and risks to historically understudied and structurally vulnerable populations. Beyond analyzing harms, the workshop invites design-oriented explorations that embed contextual integrity, data minimization, identity protection, secure delegation, and governance mechanisms directly into agentic architectures.

Call for Participation

We invite contributions across HCI, privacy, security, AI systems, design, and social computing. Submissions will be lightly reviewed to curate a diverse and constructive program.

Submission types

  • Short position papers (2–4 pages, excluding references)
  • Prototypes / provotypes (design-oriented exploratory contributions)

Suggested themes

  • Agent-mediated exposure and identity inference
  • Cross-modal leakage (text, voice, vision, sensors, telemetry)
  • Behavioral profiling, persistent memory, evolving user representations
  • Adversarial manipulation and delegated authority abuse
  • Secondary data use, governance, and cross-jurisdiction flows
  • Security/privacy by design for accountable human agency
Reviewing: Criteria emphasize clarity, interdisciplinarity, originality, and discussion value rather than only mature results.

  • Readable slide design (contrast, font size) and structured content.
