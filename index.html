SAPH-AI: Security, Agency, Privacy of Human-AI

SAPH-AI is a workshop on privacy, security, agency, identity, and governance in human-AI ecosystems, with a focus on conversational AI, generative systems, and agentic technologies.

About

SAPH-AI brings together researchers and practitioners interested in how security, agency, and privacy jointly shape human-AI interaction.

Conversational AI, generative systems, and autonomous agents are increasingly embedded in everyday communication, decision making, and digital infrastructure. These systems process sensitive data, construct behavioral profiles, and may act on behalf of users across platforms and contexts.

This workshop examines security, agency, and privacy as interconnected foundations of human-AI ecosystems. Topics include privacy risks, identity inference, multimodal data leakage, delegated authority, transparency, accountability, and privacy-centered design for conversational and agentic systems.

SAPH-AI aims to create an interdisciplinary forum for discussion across HCI, security, privacy, AI systems, social computing, and governance.

Call for Participation

We invite submissions and participation from researchers and practitioners working on privacy, security, agency, identity, and governance in human-AI systems.

Topics of Interest

  • Privacy and security risks in conversational and agentic AI
  • Identity inference, behavioral profiling, and multimodal leakage
  • Delegated decision making, user agency, and autonomy
  • Transparency, accountability, and governance of AI systems
  • Privacy-centered design, auditing, and safeguards for agentic systems
  • Human-centered evaluations of AI assistants, copilots, and autonomous agents

Workshop Format

SAPH-AI is planned as an interactive workshop combining presentations, discussion, and collaborative exchange.

  • Invited keynote or spotlight talk
  • Research paper and position paper presentations
  • Discussion sessions on emerging challenges in human-AI ecosystems
  • Collaborative exchange across security, privacy, HCI, and AI communities
