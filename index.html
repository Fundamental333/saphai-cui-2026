About

Conversational AI and autonomous agents increasingly collect, infer, and act on sensitive data. HAIP treats privacy as a first-class constraint, not an afterthought.

We examine how identities are constructed and exploited in human-agent ecosystems: behavioral inference, cross-context data reuse, and failure modes that disproportionately affect under-protected communities. We also encourage designs that operationalize contextual integrity, data minimization, and identity protection.

Call for Participation

We invite contributions across technical, social, legal, and design perspectives on identity and privacy in conversational and agentic systems.

Submission types

  • Short position papers (2–4 pages)
  • Privacy-centered provotypes (design-oriented exploratory contributions)

Suggested themes

  • Agent-mediated data exposure and identity inference
  • Cross-modal leakage (text, voice, vision, sensors)
  • Behavioral inference and sensitive attribute prediction
  • Secondary data use and governance in agentic workflows
  • Risks for structurally vulnerable or understudied populations
  • Contextual integrity, data minimization, identity protection by design
Reviewing: Submissions will be lightly reviewed by organizers for relevance and quality, to curate a diverse and constructive program.

Accessibility & Inclusion

We aim for an accessible, respectful environment and materials that don’t punish people for having eyes.

  • Accessible PDFs and clear formatting; alt text for images where applicable.
  • Readable slide design (contrast, font size) and structured content.
  • Encourage captioning and written summaries of key discussion points where feasible.
  • Multiple participation modes to support different communication styles.
  • Adheres to ACM and SIGCHI Codes of Conduct with active facilitation.
